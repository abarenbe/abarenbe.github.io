Understanding GDP and Inflation

Key Concepts

  1. Nominal GDP
  2. Real GDP
  3. Price Index
  4. Real Wages

Notation

\(P_{X,Y1}\) = Price of good X in Year 1 \(Q_{X,Y1}\) = Quantity of good X in Year 1

Calculating GDP

Example: Coffee and Tea Economy

Year Coffee Price Tea Price Coffee Quantity Tea Quantity
1 $3 $2 100 150
2 $4 $3 120 160

Nominal GDP Calculation

Year 1

\[ (P_{C,Y1} \times Q_{C,Y1}) + (P_{T,Y1} \times Q_{T,Y1}) \] \[ (3 \times 100) + (2 \times 150) = 300 + 300 = 600 \]

Year 2

\[ (P_{C,Y2} \times Q_{C,Y2}) + (P_{T,Y2} \times Q_{T,Y2}) \] \[ (4 \times 120) + (3 \times 160) = 480 + 480 = 960 \]

Real GDP Calculation (Base Year: Year 1)

Year 1

\[ (P_{C,Y1} \times Q_{C,Y1}) + (P_{T,Y1} \times Q_{T,Y1}) \] \[ (3 \times 100) + (2 \times 150) = 300 + 300 = 600 \]

Year 2

\[ (P_{C,Y1} \times Q_{C,Y2}) + (P_{T,Y1} \times Q_{T,Y2}) \] \[ (3 \times 120) + (2 \times 160) = 360 + 320 = 680 \]

Price Index

Assume average household consumes 10 coffees and 15 teas per year.

Basket Cost Calculation

Year 1: \((10 \times 3) + (15 \times 2) = 30 + 30 = 60\) Year 2: \((10 \times 4) + (15 \times 3) = 40 + 45 = 85\)

Price Index Calculation

\[ \text{Price Index} = \frac{\text{Cost of Basket in Current Year}}{\text{Cost of Basket in Base Year}} \times 100 \]

Year 1: \(\frac{60}{60} \times 100 = 100\) Year 2: \(\frac{85}{60} \times 100 \approx 141.67\)

Real Wages

Assume average annual wages: - Year 1: $30,000 - Year 2: $40,000

Can workers buy more with their wages in Year 2?

Real Wages Calculation

Baskets affordable in Year 1: \(\frac{30,000}{60} = 500\) Baskets affordable in Year 2: \(\frac{40,000}{85} \approx 470.59\)

Despite higher nominal wages, workers can buy fewer baskets in Year 2.

Real Wage Growth

Nominal Wage Growth: \(\frac{40,000}{30,000} \times 100 \approx 133.33\%\) Inflation Rate: \(\frac{141.67 - 100}{100} \times 100 \approx 41.67\%\)

Real Wage Growth: \(133.33\% - 41.67\% \approx 91.66\%\)

Real wages increased, but not as much as nominal wages.
