About Me

Education:

  • Ph.D., University of Massachusetts - Amherst
  • B.A., University of Missouri - Kansas City

My journey into economics began in high school with the Jubilee 2000 campaign, where I advocated for debt cancellation in the developing world. This early commitment to global economic justice—furthered by my participation in advocacy efforts regarding IMF structural adjustment programs—ignited my passion for understanding poverty and inequality. I deepened this commitment through service as an Americorp VISTA volunteer and at the Peace and Justice Office of the Kansas City Catholic Chancery before pursuing my degrees at the University of Missouri-Kansas City and the University of Massachusetts-Amherst.

During my studies, I helped establish a pay-day loan alternative in Kansas City and co-founded girlstoschool.org, a 501(c)3 not-for-profit that provides educational opportunities to girls in Mauritania. After completing my Ph.D., I taught for one year at Denison University before coming to Saint Martin's University.

My interests include coaching soccer, keeping up with the science of climate change, and hanging out with my family.
