About Me

Education:

  • Ph.D., University of Massachusetts - Amherst
  • B.A., University of Missouri - Kansas City

After completing high school, I worked as an Americorp VISTA volunteer and in the Kansas City Catholic Chancery Peace and Justice Office. This work left me with an interest in issues of poverty and inequality, which inspired me to study economics first at the University of Missouri-Kansas City and then at the University of Massachusetts-Amherst.

During my studies, I helped establish a pay-day loan alternative in Kansas City and set up girlstoschool.org, a 501(c)3 not-for-profit that provides educational opportunities to girls in Mauritania. After completing my Ph.D., I taught for one year at Denison University before coming to Saint Martin's University.

My interests include watching and playing soccer, keeping up with the science of climate change, and hanging out with my family.

Microeconomic Interactives

A collection of interactive models and simulations for Microeconomics students.

Unit 3: Consumer Choice

  • Optimal Choice & MRS

    Visualize the optimal consumption bundle where the budget constraint is tangent to the indifference curve.

  • Income & Substitution Effects

    Decompose the effect of a price change into income and substitution effects (Slutsky decomposition).

  • Labor Supply Derivation

    Deriving the individual labor supply curve from the labor-leisure tradeoff model.

  • Utility Surface (3D)

    Explore the 3D utility function surface to understand indifference curve mapping.

Unit 8: Cost Curves & Market Structure

  • Cost Curves Simulation

    Interactive exploration of Marginal Cost, Average Cost, and Variable Cost relationships.

  • Market Equilibrium

    Simulating market equilibrium interactions.

  • Market Equilibrium with N Firms

    See how changing the number of firms affects market outcomes and individual firm behavior.
