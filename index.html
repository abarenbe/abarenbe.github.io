About Me

Education:

  • Ph.D., University of Massachusetts - Amherst
  • B.A., University of Missouri - Kansas City

After completing high school, I worked as an Americorp VISTA volunteer and in the Kansas City Catholic Chancery Peace and Justice Office. This work left me with an interest in issues of poverty and inequality, which inspired me to study economics first at the University of Missouri-Kansas City and then at the University of Massachusetts-Amherst.

During my studies, I helped establish a pay-day loan alternative in Kansas City and set up girlstoschool.org, a 501(c)3 not-for-profit that provides educational opportunities to girls in Mauritania. After completing my Ph.D., I taught for one year at Denison University before coming to Saint Martin's University.

My interests include watching and playing soccer, keeping up with the science of climate change, and hanging out with my family.
